1. Video Frame Interpolation for Coronary Angiograms

Mar 2025 - In progress

Lowering the frame rate of coronary angiography (e.g., from 15 to 7.5 FPS) significantly reduces radiation exposure but degrades temporal resolution. We introduce Angio-FILM, a novel generative AI model for video frame interpolation (VFI) dedicated to coronary angiography. Angio-FILM uses a latent flow matching model to synthesize high-temporal-resolution (15 FPS) videos from low-frame-rate (7.5 FPS) inputs. The model was trained on 377,000 videos and validated on both internal and external datasets.

On quantitative metrics and qualitative evaluation, Angio-FILM outperformed state-of-the-art VFI methods. Furthermore, Visual Turing test involving 30 physicians and 600 videos showed accuracies of 54% in a single-video binary classification task, indicating that the generated videos were hardly distinguishable from real ones.

2. ASCVD Risk Prediction in Koreans using KAN

Jun 2024 - Under Review First author

Long-term prediction of individual atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD) risk remains a critical challenge in cardiology. We aim to develop a more accurate, and clinically applicable risk prediction model by leveraging the Kolmogorov-Arnold Network (KAN), which offers greater interpretability than MLP, to generate a simple and interpretable equation for ASCVD risk estimation.

\[ f^{\text{KAN}}(\mathbf{x}) = -a g_1(\mathrm{x}_{1}) + b g_2(\mathrm{x_{2}}) + c. \]

Contributions

  • The largest real-world data analysis on a Korean population.
  • The first application of KAN to the 10-year ASCVD risk prediction task.
  • A simple, interpretable risk prediction formula designed for clinical use.

Challenges

  • Resource: The security policies of the National Health Insurance Service (NHIS) of South Korea are highly strict. Then, data access was only permitted within a designated analysis center provided by the institution, where computational resources were extremely limited.
  • Imbalance: ASCVD events were highly imbalanced at a ratio of approximately 21:1, with a very low proportion of positive cases.
  • Evaluate: After pruning the trained network, a simplified formula was extracted and its performance was evaluated.

Solutions

  • Resource: Minimized hyperparameter tuning time by estimating appropriate network complexity through Exploratory Data Analysis. Modified parts of the official KAN code due to version mismatches in the required packages and analysis environment.
  • Imbalance: Applied weighted loss for training and used both AUROC and AUPRC for evaluation.
  • Evaluate: Developed a pipeline to convert the symbolic formula extracted from the network into a Python function using SymPy package and evaluated its output on the test dataset.

3. Data Reconstruction Method for Learning to Censored Survival Data

Jul 2022 - Jul 2023 Project Lead, First author

Classical machine learning models struggle with right-censored data in survival analysis due to incomplete target values. This study proposes a novel method that reconstructs data into pairs, aligning with the c-index structure, and represents them as matrices for classification. Our approach directly optimizes the c-index by intuitively leveraging censored data. Evaluations on real-world datasets show competitive performance with established methods.

Contributions

  • Novel matrix-based data reconstruction method to optimize the c-index directly.
  • Reformulation of survival analysis from regression to a classification problem.
  • Competitive empirical performance across SUPPORT2, AIDS3, and COLON DEATH datasets.

Challenges

  • Right-censored data do not provide complete survival times, limiting their use in standard supervised learning.
  • The original c-index is a discrete and non-differentiable metric, complicating gradient-based optimization.
  • Reconstructing all acceptable pairs into matrix form leads to exponential growth in training data size, which can become a bottleneck with larger datasets.

Solutions

  • Instead of discarding censored data, they were included by forming "acceptable pairs" based on their minimum known survival threshold and using them in the c-index. Introduced a rule-based mechanism for generating training matrices from acceptable data pairs, including handling equal survival times.
  • Derived a surrogate, differentiable version of the matrix c-index and proposed a new loss function that enables gradient descent training in neural networks.
  • Leveraged large mini-batch sizes and GPU-based optimization to handle exponential data growth from pairwise reconstruction.

5. Efficient Learning for Identifying Additional b-jets in \(t\bar{t}b\bar{b}\) Process

Sep 2020 - Mar 2022 Key Contributor

The \(t\bar{t}Hb\bar{b}\) process is essential for revealing Higgs properties but has an irreducible background from the \(t\bar{t}b\bar{b}\) process. By exploiting the special structure of \(t\bar{t}b\bar{b}\) event data, we proposed several loss functions minimized to directly increase matching efficiency for identifying additional b-jets.

Contributions

  • Demonstrated limitations of conventional binary classification approaches.
  • Redefined the identification problem by leveraging the unique structure of the \(t\bar{t}b\bar{b}\) process.
  • Proposed four surrogate loss functions (\(L_1-L_4\)) to directly optimize matching efficiency.

Challenges

  • Required to build foundational understanding of particle physics to address domain-specific problems.
  • Needed to demonstrate that the existing binary classification approach was suboptimal for this task.
  • Task-specific evaluation metric was non-differentiable, making direct optimization difficult.
  • Needed to explain the distinctive properties of each proposed model.

Solutions

  • Collaborated closely with a particle physics research group for domain knowledge and validation.
  • Designed synthetic datasets resembling real particle data structure to theoretically and empirically prove the weakness of baseline methods.
  • Analyzed the metric's properties and derived a differentiable approximation as a surrogate loss for training.
  • Created multiple data subsets based on task characteristics to evaluate and interpret model performance effectively.
